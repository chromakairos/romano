The Author

The author came into the world in Naples more than a few years ago. As soon as he was born, he cried incessantly for the first six weeks, causing his parents a whirlwind of emotions that can be synthetically described by the sequence: tenderness… worry… dismay… bewilderment… hysterical crisis… homicidal instinct.

Every attempt to calm him was in vain. The poor little one, in fact, was not tormented by any of the trivial physiological needs that torment newborns.

He had back pain...

Those acute pleas for help did not translate into an urgent need for breast milk but, rather, for an effective painkiller.

The crisis passes when his mother, in a stroke of genius, stops massaging his belly and moves to his back, finally making his lips curl into a faint smile, already veiled by the grim awareness of his bitter destiny.

He grew up happily with inseparable friends, the radiologist's son and the orthopedist's son. The only worry he remembers is that of afternoon snacks where, instead of bread and Nutella, he was forced to crunch large pills of sad calcium supplements…

After compulsory schooling, he followed what seemed like a path traced by destiny and diligently applied himself to the study and research on spinal pathologies. This little manual is the fruit of painful (in the true sense of the word) reflections and tormented (likewise) ponderings.

To tell the truth, the author asserts that since he wrote it, he has no longer had painful crises.

He becomes a little more vague when asked if this is due to the fact that he is truly cured or whether he has simply found peace with the situation...
