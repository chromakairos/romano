The Porter

Enthusiasts of this pleasant, but strenuous, position will find it difficult to practice if they are in an acute phase. The weight and anterior overhang, in fact, will severely test the resistance of suffering structures such as the lumbar muscles. For the unyielding, however, some precautions can be recommended to derive at least some therapeutic benefits from it.

The trick to making the most of the position is to lean your lower back against a radiator.

By taking the precaution of interposing at least one layer of material that cushions the heat, you can benefit from the unparalleled pain-relieving and muscle-relaxing action of exogenous thermotherapy.

The Throne

This is one of the most accessible variations, thanks to the minimal effort required to maintain the position.

A gem not to be overlooked is the possibility of using a comfortable lumbar pillow that properly orientates the correct alignment of the sagittal curves. The hands of the one who is beneath (normally the man) will be placed on the lumbar region of the one who dominates to actively perform the same action.

Pay attention to the height of the chair. One that is too low could make it difficult to return to the standing position and prolong your stay on the seat indefinitely. Fig. 21

It requires excellent physical condition and a strong sense of balance from both partners, in order to reach a happy conclusion without head trauma.

The type of advantage of this position depends on which of the two suffers from back pain.

In the case of low back pain in the male subject, the benefit is determined by the analgesic compression that the partner's closed legs exert on the painful area, especially if the symptom is localized in the sacral area.

If the pain, however, is the female subject's prerogative, the benefit is determined by the extension of the lumbar area, as already mentioned in the paragraph relating to techniques and the McKenzie method.

It is recommended to practice this variation when the symptoms have almost completely resolved to take advantage of the postural instability that characterizes it.
