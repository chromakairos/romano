This glossary provides definitions of terms used in the manual.

Antalgic Position

A posture assumed spontaneously by the body to relieve pain. In the case of back pain, it often involves a slight bending of the trunk to one side or forward.

Cabbage

The anti-inflammatory properties of cabbage have been used by many civilizations, developed in very distant eras and places. A curiosity: in the countryside of imperial Rome, compresses of urine produced by a person who had eaten cabbage were used as a pain remedy.

Disk

A cartilaginous structure located between the vertebrae, composed of an external fibrous ring and an internal gelatinous nucleus (nucleus pulposus).

Facet Joints

Small joints located at the back of the vertebrae that connect one vertebra to another and allow movement.

Herniated Disc

A condition in which the nucleus pulposus protrudes through the fibrous ring of the disc, compressing the nerve roots and causing pain.

Hymen

A thin membrane that partially or completely covers the vaginal opening.

Lumbago

Pain localized in the lumbar region (lower back).

McKenzie Method

A method of diagnosis and treatment of back pain based on the classification of symptoms and the use of specific movements (often extension) to reduce pain and restore function.

Nucleus Pulposus

The gelatinous central part of the intervertebral disc, which acts as a shock absorber.

Proprioception

The ability to perceive the position and movement of one's body in space, even without the use of sight.

Sciatica

Pain that radiates along the sciatic nerve, from the lower back down to the leg, caused by compression or irritation of the nerve roots.

Thermal Stress

A condition of stress on the body caused by exposure to extreme temperatures (cold or heat).

Therapeutic Hot Water Bottle

A small electric blanket with an electrical resistance enclosed in a fabric bag.

Exogenous Thermotherapy

The application of heat to the skin for analgesic and muscle-relaxing purposes. It differs from endogenous thermotherapy, where heat is generated directly in the tissues by the bombardment of electromagnetic waves.

Traction

A method of treating lumbago based on the principle of disc decompression obtained by applying a weight. Traction can be static or intermittent, depending on whether the weight is fixed or applied in pulses.

Cabbage (again)

The anti-inflammatory properties of cabbage have been used by many civilizations, developed in very distant eras and places. A curiosity: in the countryside of imperial Rome, compresses of urine produced by a person who had eaten cabbage were used as a pain remedy.
