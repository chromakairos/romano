McKenzie Technique

In enunciating his theory, the late New Zealand physiotherapist identified compression of sensitive nerve structures by the intervertebral disc as one of the causes of low back pain.

The proposed treatment, in this case, consists of a series of rhythmic compressions of the displaced disc portion that is compressing the nerves. These compressions on the disc are performed to seek a better positioning between the two contiguous vertebrae.

Lumbar Stabilization

This technique aims to train the holding function of the deep muscles of the spine, whose primary task is to maintain the alignment of the vertebrae.

This stabilization function is increased by finding the best vertebral alignment position and challenging it with controlled actions of minor motor stress. The progressive ability of the stabilizing muscles hinders the small and temporary displacement of the vertebrae that can generate pain.

As can be inferred from the description of the previous techniques, the handicap of this treatment is the limited possibility of making large movements once the most suitable position has been found.
