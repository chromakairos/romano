In addition to the classic methods of contraception for both sexes, some small but fundamental precautions should be added, without which the back pain sufferer would invariably face a worsening of symptoms.

The "withdrawal" method is certainly the least recommended, as the sudden flexion of the trunk, necessary for this practice, would compromise the precarious stability of the lumbar region. Fig. 8

The Diaphragm

The application of a diaphragm is also not recommended, as it would require an excessively prolonged flexion of the trunk, a movement that anyone suffering from back pain must absolutely avoid. In general, this operation is better suited to flexible individuals, while for the unfortunate person suffering from acute lumbago, it would be an insurmountable obstacle. Fig. 9

The Pill

Although of course the contraceptive pill does not directly involve mechanical stress on the spine, it can indirectly aggravate it due to the water retention the pill can induce. Water retention can cause an accumulation of liquids in the intervertebral discs, which would in turn further increase the mechanical stress to which the affected structures are subjected. Fig. 10

Worse still, the accumulation of liquids tends to also increase the pressure exerted on the pain receptors, causing an immediate worsening of symptoms.

The Condom

The difficulty of unrolling the condom directly on the penis is well-known, especially at the most crucial moment of intercourse.

A sudden, uncoordinated, and desperate movement could give the final coup de gr√¢ce to the fragile balance of the lumbar region and cause the definitive collapse of the entire structure.

Therefore, it is essential that this operation is carried out with slow and controlled movements, a condition that clashes with the urgency and desire of the moment. Fig. 11

The choice of condoms with a "stimulating effect" is also to be discouraged at these times. These condoms-- due to the presence of ribs, dots, or other "stimulating" elements-- cause additional friction and therefore excessive movement, not recommended for those suffering from back pain. Fig. 12

The use of "delaying" varieties of condom is also not advisable in these circumstances. On the other hand, clinical experience in the field has shown that pain, by its nature, is an exceptional inhibitor and that, in acute phases, the effort to bring and keep the phallic axis even a few degrees above horizontal makes the use of any device to prolong its service life counterproductive.

By contrast, for encounters in total darkness, "phosphorescent condoms" are absolutely essential. Otherwise, in case of acute lumbago, a series of repeated unsuccessful attempts to hit the target could cause further worsening of symptoms.
